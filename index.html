Hello, my name is Sarah and I am a photo fanatic! Ever since I was a young girl, photos have held a special place in my heart. From looking through antique family photo albums at grandmas house, to having my bedroom walls covered in 5x7 memories, photos have always been a part of my life. Not long after I made my grand entrance into this world, a photographer snapped a photo of baby Sarah Jowelle Kessi and gave it to my parents. That same photo, would hang on the wall for the past twenty years, reminding my parents of that little miracle.

Many photos have graced our walls over the years, marking moments to be remembered. But captured moments don’t always fill a heart with warm, fuzzy feelings. There are some photos that bring a sweet sadness. Like this photo of my Great-Grandma Tudy. Little did I know, this would be the last photo that I would have of her. And oh how I cherish it! I miss her very much, and when I see this photo, I am filled with a sadness. A sweet sadness of the memories of a special person in my life. My heart smiles.

I had never thought of choosing photography as a career until I was nearing my final years of highschool. Throughout my Senior year I listened as colleges would tell about all they had to offer. Nothing seemed to stand out to me. My ultimate passion and dream is to one day be a stay-at-home momma. To me, fulfilling this dream didn’t look like four or more years of college followed by working full-time for someone else and their schedule. The idea of choosing to make my passion for photography my career started to take shape. Photography has given me the freedom to diligently invest into my dreams of being a godly wife and mother someday. I’m doing what I love, and enjoy the people and the places that the Lord has lead me to through the camera lens. Whether it’s capturing little miracles, marking milestones, or remembering the past, I am thankful for the gift of photography and the opportunities it brings to capture what makes one’s heart full.
